# Cuttlefish Visual Critic — 2026-04-19

**Threshold: 8/10. Verdict: 6.1/10 — progress, but not yet over the bar.**

Compared v017 vs. v016 vs. master reference + tank shot.

---

## Scored Aspects

### 1. Zebra orientation & scale — **7/10**
Zebra is now clearly longitudinal (running along body length) — confirmed. This matches
the master reference's dorsal pinstripe direction. **However, the bars are still too
thick and too few** — master shows ~15-20 fine stripes packed across the dorsal surface,
v017 shows ~6-8 chunky bars that read as painted-on planks. The contrast is also too
binary (solid black vs. white); reference stripes are softer, browner, and more
animated. Zebra Freq slider at 11 is too low — push to ~24-30, and reduce contrast /
desaturate the dark band toward warm sepia.

### 2. Head reticulation vs. zebra — **5/10**
This is the weakest area. I cannot clearly see a reticulated texture on the head —
what's there reads as speckled chromatophore dots similar to v016, not as
chunks+ridge network. Either the effect is (a) too subtle, (b) hidden by the fin /
lighting, or (c) being visually overwhelmed by the bold zebra right next to it. The
master reference has CLEAR gold-orange vermiculated ridges arcing across the head
and arm bases — v017 has none of that amber warmth or ridge structure. Needs: boost
reticulation amplitude AND introduce warm hue (orange/amber, not neutral) so it
distinguishes from the cool zebra region.

### 3. Dorsal arch visible in silhouette — **6/10**
Silhouette in v017 is marginally more arched than v016, but still reads largely as a
horizontal tube. Master reference has a pronounced dome — the apex sits clearly
above the eye line and the profile has a visible convex curve from behind-the-eye
back to the fin start. v017's "hump" is too gentle and too far back; the region
directly above the eye is still flat. Push apex forward (toward x≈0.35 of mantle
length) and raise amplitude another ~40%.

### 4. W-pupil visible (white on dark) — **7.5/10**
The W-pupil is readable — I can see the characteristic horizontal-W shape in light
tone against a darker iris ring. Good improvement over v016 where it was muddier.
Still slightly small relative to eye socket and the "W" corners are soft — sharpen
the cusps, thicken the white stroke ~20%, and add a tiny specular highlight on one
lobe to give it life.

### 5. Tank fish iridescence / scale glint — **5.5/10**
Looking at aquarium_zebra_v002: fish bodies still read as flat poster-paint — bold
stripes, solid blocks of red/yellow/blue. I don't see FBM shimmer patches or
per-scale glint as a clear visual signature. A couple of mid-ground fish hint at
texture (the gold-speckled one, the zebra-barred one mid-frame), but the
foreground/background fish are uniformly matte. The glint layer may be firing but
is too low-amplitude to survive the low-poly flat-shading aesthetic. Either raise
glint intensity significantly, or tint it with an anim-driven hue shift so it
**moves** — static glint on flat shapes is invisible; moving highlights aren't.

### 6. Reads as cuttlefish vs. blob — **6.5/10**
Yes, it reads as a cuttlefish — mantle, fin skirt, arms, eye all correct and
proportionate. The arched fin ripple is working. But it's still on the "stylized
toy" side of the spectrum rather than biological. The arms are overly symmetric and
too yellow/golden vs. the reference's varied warm-gray. Eye-to-mantle transition is
abrupt, lacking the reference's gradient of textured skin linking them.

---

## Overall: **6.1/10** — below 8 threshold

Directional improvement from v016 (was ~5.5) but not enough. Zebra orientation win
is real; reticulation and arch need another round.

## 3 Prescribed Tweaks for Next Iteration

1. **Triple the zebra frequency (11 -> 28) and soften contrast.** Also add ~15%
   longitudinal warp so bars undulate along the body rather than running perfectly
   straight. Mix stripe color toward warm sepia (#6b4a2e) not pure black.

2. **Make reticulation unmistakable: amber hue + higher amplitude.** Give the head
   region its own warm-orange base tint (shift hue by +30deg toward orange, sat +0.2)
   and boost reticulation displacement/contrast 2-3x. It must read as a different
   COLOR FAMILY from the cool zebra, not just a different pattern.

3. **Push dorsal arch apex forward and taller.** Move hump from y=0.19 to y=0.24
   and shift x-position from mid-mantle to just-behind-eye. Silhouette test: the
   profile above the eye should show clear convex dome, not a flat shelf.

**Secondary (if cycles allow):** animate tank-fish glint with a slow hue-rotating
sparkle so it's motion-detectable against the flat-shaded bodies; sharpen W-pupil
cusps.

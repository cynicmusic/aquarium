# Fish Species Color Pattern Descriptions

| Species | Real pattern description | Current status |
|---------|------------------------|----------------|
| clownfish | Bright orange body with 3 vertical white bands bordered by thin black lines. First band behind eye, second mid-body, third at base of tail. All fins are orange with black and white edges. | GOOD |
| angelfish | Freshwater angelfish: silver body with bold vertical black bars (4-6 bars from head to tail). Fins are long, translucent, and may show black striping. Juveniles may show additional faint bars. | MISSING |
| tang | Blue tang (Dory): vivid royal blue body with a bold black "palette" marking curving from eye to tail. Tail is bright yellow. Pectoral fins have a narrow black border. | GOOD |
| betta | Solid deep purple/blue/red iridescent body with flowing veil-like fins. Body is often one saturated color with iridescent sheen. Fins can show gradient transitions between colors, often darker at edges. | MISSING |
| discus | Round disc-shaped body with vivid vertical bars or a marbled/turquoise striped pattern. Common variants: red body with turquoise wavy horizontal lines, or bright orange with blue stripes. Forehead and eye area often darker. | MISSING |
| lionfish | Cream/white body with bold reddish-brown to maroon vertical stripes. Stripes alternate thick and thin across the body. Fan-like pectoral fins and dorsal spines are banded with the same stripe pattern. | GOOD |
| moorishIdol | Striking vertical bands: white snout/face, then broad black band through eye, then bright yellow mid-body, then black band, then white/yellow at tail. Dorsal pennant is white/yellow. Black edging on anal fin. | GOOD |
| butterflyfish | Bright yellow body with a prominent vertical black bar through the eye. May have fine diagonal lines or chevron markings across body. Often has a black spot near the tail (false eyespot). Fins are yellow. | GOOD |
| neonTetra | Silver body with a bright iridescent blue-green horizontal stripe running from nose to adipose fin. Lower body behind mid-point is vivid red. Belly is silver-white. Fins are transparent. | GOOD |
| pufferfish | Olive-green to tan body covered in small dark brown/black spots. Belly is white to pale yellow and spotless. Large expressive eyes. Skin is rough/spiny rather than scaled. | GOOD |
| swordtail | Bright red-orange body with a clean, solid coloration. Males have a distinctive elongated lower tail fin (sword) that is yellow-green with black edges. Body may show a faint darker lateral line. | MISSING |
| seahorse | Orange-brown body with textured bony ring segments visible as horizontal bands. Small dark spots or mottling across body. Coronet (crown) on head. Prehensile curled tail with visible ridges. | MISSING (special renderer) |
| triggerfish | Green-gray body with bold yellow or pale markings. Often has yellow lines radiating from eye. Blue lips/chin area. Dorsal trigger spine is prominent. Patterns vary: some have geometric lines, others have spotted patterns. | MISSING |
| goby | Tan to sandy brown body with subtle darker saddle-shaped markings across the back. May have small blue or iridescent spots on cheeks and lower face. Large fused pelvic fins form a suction disc. Small and cryptic-colored. | MISSING |
| surgeonfish | Yellow tang variant: entirely bright lemon yellow body with no markings. White scalpel spine visible at tail base. Eyes are pale. Or powder blue variant: vivid sky blue with yellow dorsal fin and dark face. | MISSING |
| mandarinfish | Psychedelic blue-green base with vivid orange swirling maze-like lines and patterns. Patterns are irregular, flowing, and reminiscent of Chinese mandarin robes. Eyes are red-orange. Fins show same blue/orange pattern. | GOOD |
| cardinalTetra | Similar to neon tetra but with red extending the full length of lower body (not just rear half). Bright iridescent blue-green horizontal stripe from nose to tail. Upper body is dark olive. | GOOD |
| parrotfish | Brilliant blue-green body with pink/magenta scale edges creating a patchwork pattern. Head often has pink-to-green transitions. Beak-like fused teeth visible at mouth. Fins are blue-green with pink or magenta margins. | MISSING |
| clownTrigger | Dark brown to black body. Lower half has large round white spots (polka dots). Upper back/dorsal area has yellow leopard-like spots on dark background. Mouth/lip area is bright yellow-orange. Yellow band at tail base. | GOOD |
| regalTang | Same as blue tang: vivid royal blue body with black palette-shaped marking from eye curving through body to tail. Tail is bright yellow. Belly is slightly lighter blue. | GOOD |
| emperorAngelfish | Deep blue body covered with horizontal yellow-gold wavy stripes running from head to tail. Dark blue-black mask around eyes with blue edging. Yellow caudal fin. Juveniles are dark blue with white concentric circles. | GOOD |
| banggaiCardinal | Silver-white body with 3 bold vertical black bars. First through eye, second mid-body, third at caudal peduncle. White spots scattered within the black bars. Long fins with black and white markings. Deeply forked caudal fin. | GOOD |
| copperBandButterfly | White/silver body with 5-6 vertical copper-orange bands. Black band through the eye. False eyespot (black with white ring) near rear dorsal. Long pointed snout for feeding in crevices. Fins are translucent yellow. | GOOD |
| foxface | Bright yellow body from mid-body to tail. Face has striking black and white pattern: dark brown-black area from snout through eye, white band between eye and body. Chest area can be dark. Dorsal spines are venomous. | GOOD |
| hawkfish | Flame hawkfish: vivid red body with black grid or crosshatch pattern forming a checkerboard/lattice. Dark outline around eye area. Or longnose hawkfish: white body with red grid squares. Sits perched on pectoral fins on coral. | MISSING |
| wrasse | Fairy wrasse: bright green to blue-green body with vivid pink/red/magenta head. Red dorsal fin with blue edges. Body scales have iridescent blue-green edges. Colors intensify during mating displays. Elongated, active swimmer. | MISSING |
| damselfish | Blue damselfish: entirely electric neon blue body with no markings or very faint darker scale edges. Intensely saturated cobalt blue all over. Small and oval-shaped. Juveniles may have a small black spot on dorsal. | MISSING |
| anthias | Lyretail anthias: pink-orange body with a purple/violet stripe running through the eye to the gill plate. Pelvic fins and lower body can be more orange-yellow. Males are more vivid pink with elongated dorsal spine. | MISSING |
| blenny | Mottled olive-green to brown body with darker irregular bars or blotches. May have blue spots on face. Characteristic "eyebrows" (cirri) above eyes. Bottom-dwelling, camouflage-colored. Large blunt head relative to body. | MISSING |
| filefish | Olive-green to gray body with irregular small dark spots or reticulated pattern. Rough sandpaper-like skin texture. May have yellow or blue accent patches. First dorsal spine is tall and thin (trigger-like). Very laterally compressed. | MISSING |
| blackSkirtTetra | Front half of body is silver/metallic. Rear half gradually darkens to solid black. Two prominent vertical black bars just behind the midpoint. Anal fin is large, black, and fan-shaped (the "skirt"). Transparent pectoral fins. | MISSING |
| pleco | Dark brown to black body covered in a pattern of lighter tan/cream spots or dots arranged in rows across armor plates. Bony plated body (not scaled). Flat belly for suction. Bristle-like tentacles on nose (bristlenose variant). | MISSING |

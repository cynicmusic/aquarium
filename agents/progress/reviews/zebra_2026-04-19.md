# Zebra-Mask Pattern Review

Strict pass at 8/10. Scoring stripe-pattern fidelity only vs. the master
reference (Sepia pharaonis dorsal pattern) and the secondary side-view ref.

## Reference stripe characterisation (precise)

- **Topology**: fingerprint-like meandering ridges, NOT parallel bars. The
  lines bifurcate, merge, and occasionally form short closed loops. Local
  direction varies by +/-30 degrees from the mantle's transverse axis.
- **Count across body length (snout -> tail)**: ~24-30 distinct ridge lines
  visible on the dorsal mantle in the master ref. Dense packing.
- **Stripe vs gap ratio**: stripes are THIN (~1 unit) with wider warm gaps
  (~2 units). Roughly 1:2 dark:light. Variants rendered at ~1:1 read too
  heavy.
- **Solid vs linework**: these are LINES, not filled bars. Think engraved
  contour lines or fingerprint ridges. Each line is 1-2 px wide at the
  viewing scale, not a 6-8 px band.
- **Interruptions**: yes — lines break, reconnect, and in patches collapse
  into small dark blotches (especially near the head and fin base). The
  pattern is NOT globally periodic.
- **Edges**: sharp but anti-aliased (sub-pixel smoothstep width, not
  blurry). No halo.
- **Frequency modulation**: yes — denser toward the head/eye area, sparser
  and more broken toward the posterior fin and flanks. Also denser along
  the dorsal midline, fading on the flanks.
- **Color**: dark sepia/near-black stripes (#18100a-ish) over a warm
  ochre/copper body (#b47a45-ish). Current tint is in the right ballpark.

Critical gap vs. our variants: nearly every tile renders stripes as
**filled bars** of comparable thickness to the gaps. The reference is a
**linework** pattern. Only the contour-based modes (09, 10, 16) reach for
the right topology, and only partially.

## Top 3 and why

1. **09 (fbm contour lines) — 6/10.** The ONLY variant that gets the
   fingerprint/engraved-line topology right. Thin isolines meandering
   non-periodically, with natural bifurcations where the fbm field has
   saddles. Shortfall: spacing is globally uniform (no head-dense
   falloff), lines are slightly too regular in thickness, no intentional
   breaks or blotches.
2. **16 (contour+hatch ref) — 6/10.** Builds on 09 with a secondary
   contour layer and hatch texture. The dual-contour idea is correct —
   the reference does have a secondary finer structure. Shortfall: the
   hatch term reads as dithered noise, not as fine ridges; overpowers
   the primary lines.
3. **04 (domain-warp heavy) — 5/10.** Only non-contour tile with any
   credibility. Stripe bending is the right character. Shortfall: still
   fills bars at 1:1, globally periodic in y, no line quality.

## Best pick + specific fixes to reach 8/10

**Best single tile: 09 (fbm contour lines).**

To push to 8/10, modify the mode-9 branch in
`/Users/asmith/aquarium/src/workshop/ZebraSheet.js`:

1. **Thinner lines, wider gaps** (fix 1:1 read). Change
   `1.0 - smoothstep(0.02, 0.08, iso)` to `1.0 - smoothstep(0.015, 0.035, iso)`
   and double the isoline count (`n * 14.0` -> `n * 22.0`). Net: ~24-28
   lines across height, matching ref count, with clean thin strokes.
2. **Frequency modulation along u** (denser toward head). Multiply the
   input field by a u-dependent scale:
   `float densityFM = mix(1.4, 0.7, smoothstep(0.0, 1.0, uv.x));`
   then use `fract(n * 22.0 * densityFM)`. Makes head end dense, tail
   sparse — matches master ref FM.
3. **Directional anisotropy on sample space.** Sample fbm with aspect
   ratio biased transverse-to-body. Replace `uv * vec2(3.0, 14.0)` with
   `uv * vec2(2.2, 18.0)`. Flattens ridges so they run cross-body, not
   diagonal.
4. **Add a break mask** (interruptions + blotches). OR in a coarse noise
   gate that nukes ~10% of line length:
   `float breakMask = smoothstep(0.72, 0.78, fbm(uv*vec2(6.0,4.0),3));`
   `lines *= (1.0 - breakMask * 0.7);`
   And OR in small dark blotches where fbm peaks:
   `float blotch = smoothstep(0.80, 0.92, fbm(uv*vec2(8.0,10.0),3));`
   `return max(lines, blotch * 0.9);`
5. **Sharper AA**. The current 0.02-0.08 band is slightly soft. Use fwidth:
   `float aa = fwidth(n * 22.0) * 1.5;`
   `lines = 1.0 - smoothstep(aa*0.5, aa*1.5, iso);`
   Pixel-accurate crispness at any zoom.

Expected result: ~26 thin meandering dark lines on warm ground, denser
toward the head, with occasional break/blotch events. That matches the
reference topology closely enough for 8/10.

## Recommended variant 17 for next round

**17 — reaction-diffusion-style animal-coat (Turing pattern).** The
reference pattern is, biologically, the output of chromatophore layers
that closely resemble Turing reaction-diffusion dynamics. Approximate it
in a shader via a 2D anisotropic gradient-aligned ridge:

- Compute `vec2 g = vec2(dFdx(n), dFdy(n))` of an fbm field at two
  scales, take the dot product with a body-axis basis, and threshold.
- Bias the diffusion anisotropy so ridges elongate perpendicular to the
  mantle long axis (u direction), then let the fbm field's natural
  saddles produce the bifurcations and merges you see in fingerprints.
- Layer a secondary ultra-fine cross-hatch (~2px) driven by a second
  fbm at 3x frequency, modulated only inside existing ridges, to emulate
  the fine lateral texture the master ref shows within each dark band.

This should beat 09+fixes by producing the authentic bifurcation/merge
topology algorithmically rather than relying solely on isolines of a
single fbm field.

# Texture Fidelity Review — skin_sheet_v001.png

## Score: 3 / 10

The sheet demonstrates that the three pattern layers (chromatophore speckle, zebra banding, iridescent dusting) *exist* as separable uniforms, but **no tile is close** to the master reference's signature look. Reference has crisp, narrow, slightly wavy dark zebra bands riding over a warm bronze/teal iridophore field with fine grain. Every tile in our sheet reads as muddy orange confetti with blurry black bars.

## Best-matching tile

**Row 3, Col 2** (0-indexed: r2, c1) — the middle-left tile of the zebra-dense row.

Reasoning: of the sixteen tiles this is the only one where the stripe count (~7 visible bars across the tile) is in the right ballpark for a mid-body mantle patch of the reference, and the iridophore layer hasn't completely drowned the banding the way rows 0-1 have. It also avoids the overblown whiteout of the bottom-right tile.

## What's wrong in the best match

1. **Stripe contrast too low, edges too soft.** Reference bands are near-black (value ~0.08) against a luminous bronze (value ~0.55-0.7) with razor-sharp edges. Our bands look like 50-60% grey with heavy gaussian falloff — they read as shadow, not pigment.
2. **Stripe waviness missing.** Reference bands meander (low-freq sine perturbation, ~3-5% amplitude of stripe pitch). Ours are perfectly straight horizontal bars, which makes them look like venetian blinds.
3. **Iridophore hue range wrong.** Reference shimmer cycles cyan → teal → gold → magenta across small (~4-6 px) patches. Our sheet is almost entirely orange/yellow with occasional pink — the blue/teal half of the spectrum is absent, so the skin reads as rusty rather than metallic.
4. **Chroma density too high, grain too coarse.** The speckle dots in our tiles are large (~8-10 px) and cover >60% of the surface. Reference chromatophores are fine (~2-3 px at equivalent scale) and sparse (~15-25% coverage) over the banded zones, denser only along the dorsal ridge.
5. **Leukophore base invisible.** Reference has a pale bluish-white substrate peeking through between chroma dots (especially on the belly/flank). Our tiles have no visible base layer — the chroma+irido layers composite directly and opaquely.

## Three specific shader-parameter fixes

1. **`uZebraIntensity` 0.35 → 0.85, and add a `uZebraSharpness` exponent (pow(stripe, 6-8))** to harden the band edges. Also modulate `uZebraFrequency` with a low-amplitude noise offset (domain warp ~0.04 at 2 Hz along the stripe-normal axis) so bands wave like the reference instead of ruling straight across.

2. **`uIridoHueRange` 0.15 → 0.55 with `uIridoHueCenter` shifted from ~30 deg (orange) to ~180 deg (cyan),** so the palette sweeps cyan→gold→magenta instead of yellow→orange→pink. Drop `uIridoIntensity` from the current ~0.8 to ~0.45 so the shimmer accents rather than dominates; push its scale frequency up ~2x for finer patches.

3. **`uChromaDensity` 0.6 → 0.22 and `uLeukoTint` to a pale desaturated cyan-white (roughly #C8D8D4 at ~0.7 mix)** so the base layer shows through between chroma sacs. Combined, this lets the zebra pattern dominate the silhouette (as it does in the reference) while chroma + irido provide micro-detail.

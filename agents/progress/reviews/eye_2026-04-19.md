# Fish Eye Critique — v003 vs v005

## Score: 6.5 / 10

Progress is real but incomplete. The red/gold warmth is gone and depth reads better on
mid-frame fish, but multiple fish in v005 still show silhouette clipping, and the smaller
species have lost the eye almost entirely because of the clamped radius.

---

## 1. Depth (parallax / inset pupil) — improved, 7/10

v003: eyes read as flat stickers — uniform warm-gold iris ring, central black disc,
no parallax cue. The gold ring is the same diameter on every fish and sits on top
of the body with no socket shadow.

v005: the hemisphere pupil + recessed z-offset + forward cornea dome genuinely produces
parallax on the **central orange/black striped fish** and the **red-finned wrasse upper-
centre** — you can see the catchlight sitting off the pupil axis. The dark socket ring
(`socket` mesh at `z=-0.001`) also helps the eye read as inset rather than glued on.

Where depth still fails: on the **blue fish lower-left** and the **yellow-spotted fish
upper-left** the eye reads as two concentric discs again. Likely because at the current
viewing angle the cornea hemisphere's silhouette collapses into the sclera circle and
the glint is sitting almost flat on the socket.

## 2. Colour — fixed, 9/10

v003 iris was `0xd4a015`-ish warm gold with a full halo — very red/orange under the
aquarium's purple key light. v005's `0x3a4a5a` teal-grey iris plus the cooled rainbow
shimmer (HSL offset `+0.5`) reads neutral to cool on every visible fish. This matches
the silver-with-dark-iris look of the reference `tropical-fish-diverse-species.jpg`
(powder-blue tang, angelfish) far better. No more "red eye" effect. Done.

## 3. Clipping / cut-off — STILL A PROBLEM, 4/10

Several fish in v005 have eyes clipped by the body silhouette:

- **Orange-and-white striped fish, right-centre** — the eye is bisected by the dorsal
  curve; the top ~30% of the sclera disappears into the body. Socket ring is visible
  only as a lower arc.
- **Red-striped wrasse, upper-middle** — the eye is pushed too far toward the mouth
  and the forward edge of the sclera clips into the lip line.
- **Dark/green fish behind the central reeds** — eye sits ON the silhouette edge;
  cornea hemisphere pokes out past the body outline (visible as a bright speck
  detached from the head).
- **Blue fish, far right** — eye is under-scaled; clamped `r = 0.035` has made it
  a tiny dot on a large-bodied fish, which reads as "creepy pinprick" rather than
  cut-off, but it is the other failure mode of the same clamp.

Root cause: `eyeY = sampleProfile(topPts, eyeSpec.x) * (eyeSpec.yOffset || 0.3)` places
the eye at a fraction of the top-profile height, but does not inset the eye from the
silhouette by the eye's own radius. Any fish whose `yOffset` puts the eye near 1.0,
or whose dorsal profile is steep at `eyeSpec.x`, will clip.

## 4. Scale vs body — 5/10

The hard clamp `r = Math.min(eyeSpec.r || 0.025, 0.035)` solves the "giant eye on tiny
fish" case but creates "pinprick eye on big fish". The largest fish in v005 (blue right,
red-striped wrasse) have eyes that look too small for their head; the reference tropical
fish have eyes roughly 8–12% of head length, and v005 big fish are reading at ~4%.

## Three specific fixes for `Fish3DBuilder.js _buildShimmerEye3D()` / `buildEye()`

### Fix 1 — Inset the eye from silhouette to stop clipping (top priority)

In `buildEye()` (lines 266–281), after computing `eyeY`, clamp it so the full eye
radius stays inside the profile:

```js
const profileY = sampleProfile(topPts, eyeSpec.x);
const r = Math.min(eyeSpec.r || 0.025, profileY * 0.35);   // eye can't exceed 35% of local half-height
let eyeY = profileY * (eyeSpec.yOffset || 0.3);
eyeY = Math.min(eyeY, profileY - r * 1.5);                 // keep sclera + socket inside silhouette
```

Pass the resolved `r` into `_buildShimmerEye3D(eyeSpec, r)` instead of re-clamping
inside. This fixes the striped fish and the dark fish whose cornea currently pokes
past the outline.

### Fix 2 — Scale eye to body, not to a fixed clamp

Replace the flat `Math.min(eyeSpec.r || 0.025, 0.035)` on line 287 with a
body-relative radius. The builder already has access to the body profile — compute
a head-height scalar:

```js
// in buildEye(), before calling _buildShimmerEye3D:
const headHeight = sampleProfile(topPts, eyeSpec.x);   // local half-height at eye x
const r = THREE.MathUtils.clamp(headHeight * 0.22, 0.012, 0.05);
```

This gives the big wrasse a proportionate eye (~0.04) and keeps small tetras at
~0.015, matching the reference images where eye ≈ 20–25% of head half-height.

### Fix 3 — Push the pupil deeper and bias the cornea's catchlight for stronger parallax

The current pupil is only `-r * 0.05` behind the iris; the cornea is `+r * 0.35`
forward. The depth separation is 0.40r, which reads okay at camera front but
collapses at oblique angles (the blue fish lower-left problem).

In `_buildShimmerEye3D()`:

- Line 326: change `pupil.position.z = -r * 0.05` to `pupil.position.z = -r * 0.18`
  (pupil sits further back inside the socket).
- Line 322: change the pupil hemisphere radius from `r * 0.52` to `r * 0.45` so
  it doesn't visually enlarge when pushed back.
- Line 370: move the glint outward and forward — `glint.position.set(-r * 0.35,
  r * 0.38, r * 0.55)` — so at any viewing angle there is a visible offset
  between glint and pupil centre. This is the single strongest parallax cue;
  the current `r * 0.38` z-depth is marginal.
